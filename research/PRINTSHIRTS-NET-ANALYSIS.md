## üîç Domain Analysis

### Domain Characteristics

| Attribute | Assessment | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| **TLD** | .NET | Second best after .com, but significant discount |
| **EMD status** | Partial match | "Print Shirts" is a search query, but not branded |
| **Length** | 11 chars | Medium length, acceptable |
| **Memorability** | Good | Clear, descriptive, easy to spell |
| **Brandability** | Medium | Generic, not unique |
| **SEO value** | Moderate | EMD benefit exists but diminished since 2012 |
| **Commercial intent** | High | Clear commercial purpose |

### .NET vs .COM Discount

**Rule of thumb:** .NET domains typically sell for **5-15% of .com equivalent**

**Rationale:**
- .com is the default extension (users type it automatically)
- Brand trust is lower for .net
- SEO value is similar but type-in traffic is 80%+ lower
- End-user preference strongly favors .com

## üí∞ Monetization Potential

### Model 1: Print-on-Demand (Lowest Risk)

**How it works:**
- Connect to Printful/Printify API
- Customer orders on your site
- Order automatically fulfilled
- You keep margin (typically 20-40%)

**Revenue potential:**
- Average order: $25-50
- Your margin: $5-20 per order
- Breakeven: ~250-500 orders at $20 CAC

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ No inventory
- ‚úÖ No printing equipment
- ‚úÖ Scalable
- ‚úÖ Low risk

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Thin margins
- ‚ùå No control over quality
- ‚ùå Shipping times slower
- ‚ùå Heavy price competition

### Model 2: Own Equipment (Higher Risk, Higher Reward)

**How it works:**
- Buy DTG printer ($5K-20K)
- Buy blank shirts ($2-5 each)
- Print on-demand
- Fulfill yourself

**Revenue potential:**
- Average order: $25-50
- Your margin: $15-35 per order
- Breakeven: ~150-300 orders

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Better margins
- ‚úÖ Quality control
- ‚úÖ Faster shipping
- ‚úÖ Custom capabilities

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Equipment cost ($10K+)
- ‚ùå Inventory management
- ‚ùå Labor intensive
- ‚ùå Maintenance/downside risk

### Model 3: Lead Generation (Affiliate Model)

**How it works:**
- Build content site
- SEO for "print shirts" keywords
- Send traffic to Custom Ink/Printful via affiliate links
- Earn 5-20% commission

**Revenue potential:**
- 1000 visitors/month = 10-50 sales = $50-500/month
- 10K visitors/month = $500-5000/month

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Lowest risk
- ‚úÖ No fulfillment
- ‚úÖ Passive income
- ‚úÖ Scalable with SEO

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Thin margins
- ‚ùå SEO takes 6-12 months
- ‚ùå Algorithm dependency
- ‚ùå Commission cuts risk

### Model 4: Flip to Printer

**How it works:**
- Build site, get traffic
- Sell lead-gen site to local print shop
- Or sell to larger competitor

**Exit potential:**
- Local print shop: $5K-20K (500+ leads/month)
- Larger player: $10K-50K (established revenue)

**Timeline:** 12-24 months

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Key Risks

### Domain-Specific Risks

1. **.NET discount** - 85-95% value reduction vs .com
2. **Trademark risk** - "Print Shirts" is generic (low risk)
3. **EMD depreciation** - Google reduced EMD benefit in 2012
4. **Type-in traffic** - Minimal (<5 type-ins/month likely)
5. **Brand limitations** - Hard to build unique brand on generic term

### Market Risks

1. **Oversaturation** - Thousands of competitors
2. **Price pressure** - Margin compression
3. **CAC inflation** - Facebook/Google ads getting expensive
4. **POD platform dominance** - Printful/Printify control supply
5. **Low switching costs** - Customers can easily switch

### Execution Risks

1. **SEO competition** - "Custom t-shirts" KD 80+
2. **Content costs** - $5K-20K for quality content
3. **Time to profitability** - 12-24 months for SEO
4. **Technical integration** - POD API setup (2-4 weeks)
5. **Customer support** - Returns, sizing issues, complaints
