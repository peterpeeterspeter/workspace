### H2: What Is Crash Gambling? (The Basics)

**Section 1: Simple Definition (150 words)**
- Crash = a multiplier game
- Multiplier starts at 1.00x, climbs rapidly, then "crashes" (round ends)
- Your goal: Cash out BEFORE crash
- Cashout at 2x = 2x your bet
- Crash at 1.5x (you didn't cash out) = lose your bet

**Section 2: How a Crash Round Works (Step-by-Step) (250 words)**
1. **Betting phase (5-10 seconds):** Place your bet, set auto-cashout (optional)
2. **Round starts:** Multiplier begins climbing from 1.00x
3. **The climb:** Multiplier goes up (1.1x, 1.5x, 2x, 3x, 5x, 10x...)
4. **The crash:** At random point, multiplier crashes (round ends)
5. **Winners:** Players who cashed out before crash
6. **Losers:** Players who didn't cash out (or cashed out AFTER crash)

### H2: Crash Gambling Math Explained (Simply)

**Section 1: The Multiplier (150 words)**
- Multiplier = how much you'll win if you cash out
- 2x = double your money
- 10x = 10x your money
- **Probability:** Higher multipliers = less likely
- Example: 50% chance of reaching 2x, 10% chance of reaching 10x

**Section 2: House Edge & RTP (200 words)**
- **House edge:** Casino's advantage (usually 3-5%)
- **RTP (Return to Player):** 95-97% (you get back 95-97% of all bets long-term)
- **What this means:** For every $100 bet, you'll win back $95-97 over time
- **Example:** Bet $100 at 2x auto-cashout
  - 50% of time: Win $200
  - 50% of time: Lose $100
  - Long-term: Lose $5 (house edge)

**Table: Crash Game RTP Comparison**
| Game | RTP | House Edge |
|------|-----|------------|
| Crash (typical) | 95-97% | 3-5% |
| Blackjack (perfect play) | 99.5% | 0.5% |
| Roulette | 94-97% | 3-6% |
| Slots | 85-95% | 5-15% |

**Section 3: Probability of Crashes (200 words)**
- Crash is RANDOM (each round independent)
- **Probability formula:** P(multiplier ‚â• x) = 1/x
- Examples:
  - 50% chance of reaching 2x
  - 33% chance of reaching 3x
  - 10% chance of reaching 10x
  - 1% chance of reaching 100x

**Table: Crash Multiplier Probabilities**
| Multiplier | Chance of Reaching | Risk Level |
|------------|-------------------|------------|

**Key takeaway:** Low multipliers = more frequent wins (smaller profits). High multipliers = rare wins (bigger profits).
