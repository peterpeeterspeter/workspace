## What is No-KYC Gambling? (Privacy Explained)

**KYC (Know Your Customer)** is the identity verification process most casinos use: scan your passport, snap a selfie, upload proof of address. It's invasive, unnecessary, and a data breach waiting to happen.

**No-KYC gambling** skips all that. You sign up with nothing more than an email (or sometimes not even that), deposit crypto, and play. No ID scans. No selfies. No database with your personal info waiting to be hacked.

### How No-KYC Casinos Operate

**Crypto-only payments:** By accepting cryptocurrency only, no-KYC casinos avoid the banking regulations that mandate identity verification. Traditional payment processors (Visa, PayPal) require KYC ‚Äî crypto doesn't.

**Anonymous accounts:** Instead of identity verification, these casinos track you by:
- Email address (or username)
- Wallet address (for deposits/withdrawals)
- Session cookies

**The trade-off:** No-KYC casinos often have higher deposit/withdrawal limits before triggering mandatory KYC (usually $2,000-$15,000 depending on the casino). Stay under those limits, and you're fully anonymous.

### Is No-KYC Legal?

**Yes.** There's no law requiring *players* to verify their identity. KYC requirements come from:
- Payment processors (banks, Visa, etc.) ‚Äî bypassed with crypto
- Casino license requirements (varies by jurisdiction)

No-KYC casinos typically operate under licenses that don't mandate KYC or simply choose to accept the legal risk. From a *player* perspective, accessing no-KYC casinos is legal in most jurisdictions (with some exceptions like Washington state).

**Our stance:** Privacy is a right. No-KYC gambling is no different than paying cash at a casino ‚Äî anonymous, legal, nobody's business.
