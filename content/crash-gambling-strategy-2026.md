## Understanding Crash Gambling

**Crash games are designed with a 1% house edge.**
- Every $1 bet = Expected loss of $0.01 (1%)
- Long-term: You **cannot** beat the game
- Short-term: Variance creates winners and losers
- **Strategies manage variance**, they don't overcome house edge

**Example:**
- Bet $1 at 1.5x target
- Win probability: 66% (you win 2 out of 3)
- Average payout: $1 × 0.66 = $0.66
- Expected loss per bet: $1 - $0.66 = -$0.34 (loss of 34%)
- **Over 100 bets:** Expected loss = $34

**Key insight:** Strategies optimize fun and bankroll longevity, not profits.

### Strategy 2: Martingale (NOT Recommended)

**How it works:**
- Start with $1 bet
- After loss: Double to $2
- After loss: Double to $4
- After loss: Double to $8
- Continue until win (then reset to $1)

**Why it FAILS:**
- **Table limits** prevent infinite doubling
- **Bankroll wipeout:** 7 losses in a row = $1+2+4+8+16+32+64+128 = $255 lost
- **No recovery** possible (can't double further due to limits)
- **Guaranteed loss** long-term (house edge + table limits)

**Example disaster:**
- Start: $100 bankroll
- Lose 7 rounds: $1, $2, $4, $8, $16, $32, $64, $128 (cumulative: $255)
- **Next bet would be $256** (exceeds table limit)
- **Bankrupt:** Cannot continue

**Verdict:** **AVOID Martingale.** It's a path to guaranteed ruin.

### Strategy 3: Fibonacci (Moderate Risk)

**How it works:**
- Follow Fibonacci sequence: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21...
- After loss: Move to next bet in sequence
- After win: Reset to beginning of sequence

**Example:**
- Bet 1: Lose
- Bet 1: Lose
- Bet 2: Lose
- Bet 3: Win → Reset
- Bet 1: Lose
- Bet 1: Lose
- Bet 2: Win → Reset

**Why it's better than Martingale:**
- Slower bet escalation (Fibonacci vs. doubling)
- **Still risky** (can hit table limits or lose big)

**Verdict:** **NOT recommended** for crash games. Flat betting is superior.

### Strategy 4: Percentage-Based Betting

**How it works:**
- Bet percentage of bankroll based on confidence
- Example: 1% when confident, 0.5% when cautious
- Adjusts as bankroll grows/shrinks

**Formula:**
```
bet_amount = bankroll × risk_percentage
```

**Risk percentages:**
- **Conservative:** 0.5-1% (play longer)
- **Moderate:** 1-2% (standard play)
- **Aggressive:** 3-5% (high risk, high reward)

**Why it can work:**
- Adapts to bankroll size
- Maintains consistent relative risk
- **Prevents overbetting** when winning
- **Prevents underbetting** when losing

**Example:**
- $100 bankroll, 1% risk = $1 bet
- Bankroll grows to $200, 1% risk = $2 bet
- Bankroll drops to $50, 1% risk = $0.50 bet

**Verdict:** Useful for experienced players who understand risk tolerance.

### Strategy 5: D'Alembert System (Aggressive)

**How it works:**
- Start with sequence: 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 30, 49...
- After loss: Move to next number in sequence
- After win: Drop back two numbers in sequence

**Sequence example:**

- 21, 30 (win) → drop back to 8, bet 13

**Example disaster:**

**Verdict:** **NEVER use D'Alembert.** Guaranteed path to ruin.
