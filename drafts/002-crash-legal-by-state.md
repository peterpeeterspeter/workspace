## Is Crash Gambling Legal in the US? (Federal Overview)

**Short answer:** It's complicated. Federal law doesn't explicitly ban online crash gambling, but state laws vary wildly.

### Federal Laws That Matter

**UIGEA (2006) — Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act**
- **What it does:** Makes it illegal for banks to process **illegal** online gambling transactions
- **What it doesn't do:** Doesn't define what "illegal" gambling is (leaves that to states)
- **Key point:** UIGEA targets **payment processors**, not individual players

**Wire Act of 1961**
- **Original intent:** Stop organized crime (sports betting via telephone)
- **2010 DOJ opinion:** Applied to all online gambling
- **2019 DOJ reversal:** Wire Act only applies to sports betting
- **Current status:** Legal gray area, but generally safe for non-sports gambling

**SAFE Ports Act (2006)**
- Attached UIGEA as a rider
- Unrelated to gambling (port security)
- Just the vehicle UIGEA used to pass

**Bottom line:** Federal law doesn't ban you from playing crash games. State law is what matters.
