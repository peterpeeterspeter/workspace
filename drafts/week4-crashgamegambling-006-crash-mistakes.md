## Beginner Mistakes (1-5)

### Mistake #1: No Auto-Cashout Set

**The error:** Manual cashout ‚Üí timing delays ‚Üí missed multiplier.

**How it happens:**

You plan to cash out at 2x. The multiplier climbs: 1.5x... 1.8x... 1.95x... You reach for the button, but lag kicks in. By the time you click, the game crashed at 1.01x. You lost everything.

**The cost:**

You miss 50% of your intended profit. If you planned to make $100 profit at 2x, you now lose $100. That's a **$200 swing** per mistake.

**The fix:**

**ALWAYS set auto-cashout.** It removes human error, reduces lag, and eliminates greed. Set it once, trust it.

**EV impact:** -50% expected profit per missed multiplier. Over 100 rounds, that's thousands lost.

### Mistake #2: Betting Your Entire Balance

**The error:** Deposit $100, bet $100 on one round.

**How it happens:**

You deposit, see the crash game climbing to 5x, 10x, and think "I can turn $100 into $1,000!" You bet everything. The game crashes at 1.00x. Your bankroll is gone in one second.

**The cost:**

100% bankroll ruin. One bad round = you're broke.

**The math:**

If you bet 100% of bankroll, you need a 100% win rate to break even. Crash gives you ~47% win rate (at 2x target). You **will** go broke.

**The fix:**

Never bet more than **1-5% of bankroll per round**. If you have $100, bet $1-5 max. This absorbs variance and gives you 100+ rounds to find a winning streak.

**EV impact:** Betting 100% = 100% ruin probability within 50 rounds.

### Mistake #3: Chasing Losses Immediately

**The error:** Lose $50, bet $100 to "win it back."

**How it happens:**

You lose a few rounds in a row. Frustration builds. You think "I'm due for a win" (fallacy ‚Äî see Mistake #6). You double your bet to recover losses. You lose again. Now you're down $200. You bet $400...

**The cost:**

Chasing losses **doubles your expected loss** per session. Instead of losing 3% EV (house edge), you're losing 6%+ due to emotional tilt.

**The math:**

- Normal betting: $10 per round √ó 3% house edge = $0.30 loss per round
- Chasing losses: $20, $40, $80 bets ‚Üí average $50 per round √ó 3% = $1.50 loss per round
- **5x more loss per round**

**The fix:**

Walk away after a loss. Take a **minimum 30-minute break**. Clear your head. Only return when you're calm.

**EV impact:** 2x expected loss per session when chasing.

### Mistake #5: No Max Loss Limit

**The error:** Play until you're broke.

**How it happens:**

You deposit $200, plan to play "for a while." Hours pass, you're down $150, but you keep thinking "one more round, I'll turn it around." Eventually, you lose the remaining $50. You're broke.

## Intermediate Mistakes (6-10)

### Mistake #6: The "I'm Due" Fallacy

**The error:** "I lost 5 rounds in a row, I'm due for a win."

**The reality:**

Each crash round is **independent**. The game doesn't remember past results. If you lost 5 rounds, the 6th round still has a 47% win rate (at 2x target). You're not "due" for anything.

**The cost:**

Betting bigger to "catch up" ‚Üí bigger losses ‚Üí faster bankroll ruin.

**The math:**

If you bet $10 normally, then "catch up" with $50 bets after losses:
- Normal EV: $10 √ó 3% house edge = $0.30 loss per round
- "Catch up" EV: $50 √ó 3% = $1.50 loss per round
- **5x more loss when chasing**

**The fix:**

Accept that each round is independent. Past results don't influence future outcomes. Bet the same amount every round (fixed-wager strategy).

**EV impact:** Chasing "due" wins increases loss per round by 5x.

### Mistake #7: Cashing Out Too Late (Greed)

**The error:** Auto-cashout at 2x, you see 3x and cancel to chase 5x.

**How it happens:**

You set auto-cashout at 2x. The multiplier passes 2x... 2.5x... 3x... Greed kicks in. You think "I can get 5x!" You cancel auto-cashout. The game crashes at 1.05x. You lose everything.

**The cost:**

Instead of winning $100 (at 2x), you lose $100. That's a **$200 swing** due to greed.

**The math:**

- Auto-cashout at 2x: 47% win rate ‚Üí EV = -$3 per $100 bet
- Manual cashout (greed): 20% win rate (targeting 5x) ‚Üí EV = -$20 per $100 bet
- **7x more loss when greedy**

**The fix:**

**Set auto-cashout and NEVER touch it.** Trust your strategy. Don't let greed override math.

**EV impact:** Canceling auto-cashout increases loss per round by 7x.

### Mistake #8: Cashing Out Too Early (Fear)

**The error:** Auto-cashout at 1.5x when you planned 3x.

**How it happens:**

You set auto-cashout at 3x. But after a few losses, fear takes over. You lower it to 1.5x "to be safe." Now you're winning more often, but your profit per win is tiny. Long-term, you lose due to house edge.

**The cost:**

Slower bankroll growth + higher house edge impact.

**The math:**

- Auto-cashout at 3x: 33% win rate, $200 profit per win
- Auto-cashout at 1.5x: 65% win rate, $50 profit per win
- Both have negative EV, but 1.5x drains bankroll slower (more rounds = more house edge)

**The fix:**

Trust your strategy. If you planned 3x, stick to 3x. Don't let fear override math.

**EV impact:** Lowering cashout target reduces profit per win by 75%.

### Mistake #10: No Strategy, Just "Feel"

**The error:** Betting random amounts, cashing out randomly.

**How it happens:**

You sit down, bet random amounts ($5, $20, $100), and cash out whenever you "feel like it." Sometimes you aim for 2x, sometimes 10x. You have no plan, no math, no consistency.

**The cost:**

Volatile swings, no edge, faster losses.

**The math:**

Random betting has no strategy. You're just gambling, which means the house edge will grind you down over time.

**The fix:**

Use a **math-backed strategy**:
- **Fibonacci:** Increase bets after losses (sequence: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8...)
- **Fixed-wager:** Bet same amount every round ($10)
- **Conservative:** Auto-cashout at 1.5x, bet 2% of bankroll

**EV impact:** No strategy = maximum variance + guaranteed long-term loss.

### Mistake #12: Variance Blindness

**The error:** "I won $500 in one hour, I'm profitable!"

**The reality:**

You'll lose it back next week. Variance swings both ways. Short-term wins mean nothing.

**The math:**

In crash, you can get lucky and win 10 rounds in a row. But over 1,000+ rounds, the house edge will prevail. Your true win rate is ~47% (at 2x target).

**The cost:**

Withdrawing too early, thinking you're a "pro." Quitting your job (bad idea). Bragging to friends (embarrassing when you lose it back).

**The fix:**

Track results over **1,000+ rounds**, not 100. Calculate actual RTP. If it's 96-98%, you're normal. If it's above 105%, you're lucky (and it won't last).

**EV impact:** Variance blindness leads to false confidence + bigger bets = faster ruin.

**The fix:**

**EV impact:** Fatigue increases mistakes by 3x in hour 3+.
