## Gambler's Fallacy: Crash Edition

**Gambler's Fallacy** is the mistaken belief that if something happens more frequently than normal during a given period, it will happen less frequently in the future (to "balance out").

**Crash examples:**

**‚ùå "After ten 1.5x crashes, a 10x is due!"**
**Reality:** The probability of hitting 10x is exactly the same as always (~9%). Previous rounds don't matter.

**‚ùå "The game is rigged, it hasn't hit 2x in 20 rounds!"**
**Reality:** The probability of hitting 2x is still ~50%. It's variance, not rigging.

**‚ùå "Just hit three 100x multipliers, the game is 'hot'!"**
**Reality:** The probability of hitting 100x is still ~1%. "Hot" and "cold" are human narratives, not math.

**Why the fallacy feels true:**
- Small samples create "streaks" (flip a coin 10 times, you'll often get runs of 3-4 heads or tails)
- We notice streaks, ignore the randomness around them
- We create stories to make sense of chaos

**The math doesn't care about your story.** Every round is a fresh coin flip.
