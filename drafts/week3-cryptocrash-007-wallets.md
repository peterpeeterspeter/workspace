## Wallet Types: What's the Difference?

Before we recommend wallets, understand the types:

### Hot Wallets vs Cold Wallets

**Hot Wallets (Online):**
- Connected to the internet (software wallets, mobile apps, browser extensions)
- **Pros:** Convenient, fast transactions, easy to use
- **Cons:** Higher hack risk (if your device is compromised)
- **Best for:** Small amounts (<$1,000), frequent deposits/withdrawals

**Cold Wallets (Offline):**
- Not connected to the internet (hardware wallets, paper wallets)
- **Pros:** Maximum security (hackers can't access offline devices)
- **Cons:** Less convenient, slower to use
- **Best for:** Large amounts (>$1,000), long-term storage

**For crash gambling:** Use a hot wallet for daily play and a cold wallet for long-term holdings.

### Custodial vs Non-Custodial

**Custodial Wallets:**
- Third party holds your private keys (e.g., exchange wallets like Binance, Coinbase)
- **Pros:** Easy recovery (if you lose password, support can help)
- **Cons:** Third party can freeze your account, not your keys not your crypto
- **Best for:** Beginners who might lose their private keys

**Non-Custodial Wallets:**
- You hold your private keys (e.g., MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Ledger)
- **Pros:** Full control, no third party can freeze your funds
- **Cons:** If you lose your private keys, your crypto is gone forever
- **Best for:** Privacy-conscious players, experienced crypto users

**For crash gambling:** Non-custodial is better for privacy (no paper trail to an exchange).

## Wallet Security Best Practices

Whether you choose a hot or cold wallet, follow these security rules:

### 1. Never Share Your Private Keys

**Private keys** = 12-24 word recovery phrase. If someone has your private keys, they can steal your crypto.

**Do:**
- Write down your private keys on paper (store offline)
- Keep multiple copies (home, safe, bank box)
- Never share them with anyone (not even casino support)

**Don't:**
- Store them in screenshots, notes apps, or emails
- Share them with "customer support" (scammers posing as support)
- Enter them on fake websites (phishing scams)

### 2. Enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)

If your wallet supports 2FA, enable it.

**Best 2FA apps:** Google Authenticator, Authy, YubiKey

**Avoid:** SMS 2FA (vulnerable to SIM swapping attacks)

### 3. Use Separate Wallets for Gambling

**Smart strategy:** Use one wallet for crash gambling, another for long-term holdings.

**Why:** If your gambling wallet gets compromised (hack, casino scam), your main holdings are safe.

**Example:**
- **Gambling wallet:** Trust Wallet with $200-$500
- **Main wallet:** Ledger Nano X with $5,000+ (offline storage)

### 4. Don't Leave Large Amounts on Casinos

Casinos are **not** banks. If they get hacked, go bankrupt, or block your account, you could lose everything.

**Best practice:**
- Keep only what you're actively gambling on the casino ($50-$200)
- Withdraw winnings to your private wallet
- Don't treat casinos as savings accounts

### 5. Double-Check Addresses Before Sending

Crypto transactions are irreversible. If you send to the wrong address, your funds are gone.

**Before sending:**
- Verify the first 4 and last 4 characters of the address
- Send a small test transaction first ($1-$5)
- Confirm the deposit arrived before sending more
