## How Crash Games Actually Work

Crash games use a system called **provably fair technology**. Here's how it works in plain English:

**Two inputs create the crash multiplier:**
- **Server seed:** Known only to the casino (generated before the round)
- **Client seed:** Chosen by you (the player)

These combine through a cryptographic hash function to determine exactly when the crash will happen.

**The sequence:**
1. Before you play, the casino shows you the **hashed server seed** (encrypted version)
2. You provide your **client seed** (or use a random one)
3. The game combines both seeds through SHA-256 hashing
4. The result determines the crash multiplier

**Why this matters:** If the casino wanted to rig the game, they'd need to change the server seed AFTER showing you the hash. That's mathematically impossible with modern encryption.

## Red Flags: 5 Signs of a Rigged Crash Game

### 1. No Provably Fair Verification
**Red flag:** Casino won't show you server seeds or let you verify rounds.
**What it means:** They control the RNG and could be manipulating multipliers.
**Verdict:** Avoid.

### 2. Unrealistic Multiplier Patterns
**Red flag:** You see the same multipliers repeating (e.g., always crashing at 1.00x, 2.00x, 10.00x).
**What it means:** Predetermined patterns, not random.
**Verdict:** Suspicious—test other games.

### 3. Lack of Licensing
**Red flag:** No license from MGA, Curacao, UKGC, or other authorities.
**What it means:** No oversight, no recourse if they refuse payouts.
**Verdict:** High risk—avoid for real money.

### 4. Delayed or Denied Payouts
**Red flag:** Winning withdrawals take weeks or get "cancelled" without explanation.
**What it means:** They might be cash-flow insolvent or intentionally stalling.
**Verdict:** Warning sign—read reviews.

### 5. Fake Reviews or No Community
**Red flag:** Only 5-star reviews on their own site, no discussions on Reddit/forums.
**What it means:** They're suppressing negative feedback.
**Verdict:** Do your research elsewhere.
