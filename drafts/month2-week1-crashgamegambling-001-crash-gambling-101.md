## What Is Crash Gambling? The Simple Explanation

Crash gambling is a fast-paced casino game where **you bet on how high a multiplier will climb before it crashes.**

1. **Place your bet** (any amount from $0.10 to thousands)

## Crash Gambling Math: The Basics

### House Edge: The Casino's Advantage

Every crash game has a **house edge** ‚Äî a mathematical advantage for the casino.

**Typical house edge: 1-4%**

This means:
- For every $100 wagered, the casino expects to keep $1-4
- Your expected return is $96-99 for every $100 bet

**Example:** A crash game with 1% house edge:
- You bet $10 on 2.00x multiplier
- Your actual win probability is **49.5%** (not 50%)
- Over 1,000 rounds, you'll lose ~$10 on average

**This isn't rigging ‚Äî it's math.** All casino games have a house edge.

### Crash Multiplier Probability

The probability of hitting a certain multiplier follows a simple formula:

**Probability = 1 / Multiplier**

**Examples:**
- **1.50x multiplier:** 66.7% chance of hitting (2 in 3)
- **2.00x multiplier:** 50% chance of hitting (1 in 2)
- **5.00x multiplier:** 20% chance of hitting (1 in 5)
- **10.00x multiplier:** 10% chance of hitting (1 in 10)
- **100x multiplier:** 1% chance of hitting (1 in 100)

**Key Insight:** Lower multipliers hit more often. Higher multipliers hit less often.

### Expected Value (EV): Your Long-Term Outcome

**Expected Value** is what you can expect to win (or lose) per bet on average.

**Formula:**
```
EV = (Win Probability √ó Win Amount) - (Loss Probability √ó Loss Amount)
```

**Example:** Betting $10 on 2.00x multiplier with 1% house edge:
- Win probability: 49.5%
- Win amount: $10 ($20 payout - $10 bet)
- Loss probability: 50.5%
- Loss amount: $10

**EV = (0.495 √ó $10) - (0.505 √ó $10) = -$0.10**

You'll lose **10¬¢ per $10 bet** on average.

**Over 1,000 bets at $10 each:** Expected loss = **$100**
