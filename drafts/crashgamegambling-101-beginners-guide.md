## What Is Crash Gambling?

**Crash gambling** is a simple but thrilling casino game where you bet on how high a multiplier will climb before it "crashes."

**The basic concept:**
1. You place a bet (any amount)
2. A multiplier starts climbing: 1.00x → 1.50x → 2.00x → 3.00x...
3. You cash out BEFORE the crash
4. If you cash out: You win your bet × multiplier
5. If it crashes before you cash out: You lose your bet

**Example:**
```
Bet: $10
Multiplier climbs: 1.00x → 1.50x → 2.00x → 2.50x → CRASH!
You cash out at 2.00x: Win $20 ($10 profit) ✅
You wait too long: Lose $10 ❌
```

**That's it.** Simple rules, fast action, big potential wins.

## How Crash Games Work: The Math Behind the Multiplier

Crash games use **provably fair algorithms** based on cryptographic hashing:

**Two inputs create the crash point:**
1. **Server seed** — generated by the casino (hidden)
2. **Client seed** — chosen by you (or random)

These combine through SHA-256 hashing to produce a crash multiplier.

**The process:**
1. Before the round, the casino shows you the **hashed server seed** (encrypted)
2. You see the multiplier climbing in real-time
3. You decide when to cash out
4. After the crash, the casino reveals the actual server seed
5. You can verify the round was fair using online tools

**Why this matters:** It proves the casino can't manipulate the crash point mid-round. The outcome is determined BEFORE the round starts.

**The #1 mistake beginners make:** Betting too much per round.

**Example:**

**Why this matters:** Even with 99% RTP, variance can bust you. Proper bankroll management keeps you in the game.

## Crash Gambling Terminology

**Multiplier:** The climbing number (1.00x, 2.00x, 3.00x...)
**Crash point:** When the round ends (game over)
**Auto-cashout:** Automatic sell order at preset multiplier
**Provably fair:** Cryptographic proof of fair gameplay
**House edge:** Casino's advantage (usually 1%)
**RTP:** Return to player percentage (usually 99%)
**Volatility:** Risk level (how much results swing)
**Bankroll:** Total gambling funds
**Round:** One complete game (bet → climb → crash)
