## What is No-KYC Gambling? (Privacy Explained)

### The Problem: Most Casinos Demand Your Identity

**Typical KYC (Know Your Customer) requirements:**
- Government ID (passport, driver's license)
- Selfie with ID (prove it's you)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
- Source of funds (where'd you get the money?)
- Phone verification (SMS confirmation)

**Why they do it:**
- Legal requirement (in regulated jurisdictions)
- Anti-money laundering (AML) compliance
- Fraud prevention
- Age verification

**The privacy cost:** Your data sits on their servers. Forever. Vulnerable to breaches, subpoenas, insider threats.

### The Solution: No-KYC Crash Casinos

**No-KYC means:**
- **No ID verification:** No passport, no selfie
- **No proof of address:** No utility bills
- **No phone verification:** No SMS required (sometimes)
- **Anonymous accounts:** Email only (or no email)
- **Crypto-only:** Bypasses traditional finance surveillance

**How they operate:**
- Licensed in crypto-friendly jurisdictions (Curacao, Costa Rica, Panama, Anjouan)
- No requirement to collect ID (local laws don't mandate it)
- Cryptocurrency transactions (no fiat, no banking integration)
- Privacy-first business model

**The trade-off:**
- **Pros:** Complete privacy, anonymity, no data leaks
- **Cons:** No responsible gambling tools (can't self-exclude if no ID), higher limits (problem gambling risk)
