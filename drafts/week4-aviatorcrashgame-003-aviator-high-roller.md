## Psychology of High-Stakes Crash

### The Dopamine Hit

**Winning $5k in one round feels amazing.** Your brain floods with dopamine. You feel invincible.

**The pain of losing $10k in one minute hurts more.** Emotional devastation, anger, desperation.

**Gambler's fallacy:** "I lost 5 rounds in a row, I'm due for a win." **False.** Each round is independent (50/50 regardless of past results).

### Chasing Losses (The #1 Way High Rollers Go Broke)

**How it happens:**
1. Lose $10k
2. Feel angry, desperate to "win it back"
3. Increase bets to $2k-3k
4. Lose more
5. Increase bets to $5k
6. Crash at 1.00x
7. Go broke

**Discipline:** Set stop-loss (10% of bankroll). When you hit it, **STOP.** Walk away. Come back tomorrow.

### Emotional Discipline: Walk Away When You're Up

**Hardest skill:** You're up $20k. You feel amazing. You want to keep playing. **STOP.**

**Why:** Variance swings both ways. If you keep playing, you'll give it back.

**Rule:** Set win goal (20% of bankroll). When you hit it, **WITHDRAW.** Lock in profit.

**Example:** $50k bankroll, win goal = $10k profit. When you're at $60k, withdraw $10k. Now you're playing with house money.

### Reality Check: If You Can't Afford to Lose $50k, You're Not a High Roller

**True high roller:** Can lose $50k without impacting life (can afford to lose it).

**Fake high roller:** Borrows money, sells assets, tilts retirement funds to gamble.

**If you're stressed about losses:** You're over-betting. Reduce bet size or quit.
