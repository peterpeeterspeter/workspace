## The 10-Point Security Checklist

Before you deposit **any** money, verify these 10 security features:

### 1. Valid Gambling License

**What to look for:**
- License number in the footer (e.g., "Licensed by Curaçao Gaming #12345")
- Clickable link to the regulator's website
- License verification works (check the regulator's database)

**Top licenses:**
- **MGA (Malta Gaming Authority):** Gold standard. Strict regulations, player protection.
- **UKGC (UK Gambling Commission):** Most reputable, but only for UK players.
- **Curaçao (Gaming Authority):** Common for crash casinos. Basic regulation, better than nothing.

**Red flags:**
- No license listed
- Fake license number (verify on regulator's website)
- License from unrecognized jurisdictions

**How to verify:** Copy the license number and search it on the regulator's official website.

### 2. SSL Encryption (HTTPS)

**What to look for:**
- **HTTPS** in the URL (not HTTP)
- Padlock icon in your browser's address bar
- Valid SSL certificate (click the padlock to check)

**Why it matters:** SSL encrypts your data (passwords, payments) so hackers can't steal it.

**How to check:** Click the padlock → "Connection is secure" → Certificate is valid.

**Red flag:** "Connection is not secure" warning. Close the site immediately.

### 3. Provably Fair Games

**What to look for:**
- "Provably fair" section on the crash game page
- Seed hash verification tool
- Client seed and server seed displayed before each round

**How it works:**
- Casino publishes a **server seed** (hashed, so you can't see it)
- You provide a **client seed** (you choose it)
- After the round, the casino reveals the server seed
- You verify the crash result was truly random

**Why it matters:** Without provably fair systems, casinos can rig crash games to crash at 1.00x more often than they should.

**Red flags:**
- No provably fair information
- Can't verify results
- Crash consistently crashes at suspicious multipliers (e.g., 1.00x, 1.01x repeatedly)

### 4. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

**What to look for:**
- 2FA option in account settings
- Support for authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy)
- Email verification as backup

**Why it matters:** If someone steals your password, 2FA stops them from accessing your account.

**How to enable:** Go to Settings → Security → Enable 2FA → Scan QR code with authenticator app.

**Red flag:** Casino doesn't offer 2FA. Your account is at risk.

### 5. Cold Storage for Funds

**What to look for:**
- Casino mentions "cold storage" in security documentation
- Majority of funds stored offline (not in hot wallets)
- Insurance for hot wallet breaches (some casinos offer this)

**Why it matters:** Hot wallets (online) are vulnerable to hacks. Cold storage (offline) is much harder to steal.

**Red flag:** Casino stores all funds in hot wallets. If they get hacked, you could lose everything.

### 6. Independent Audits

**What to look for:**
- Audit certificates from third-party testing labs (eGaming, iTech Labs, GLI)
- RNG certification (proves crash games are random)
- Payout percentage reports (RTP audits)

**Top auditors:**
- **eGaming (eCOGRA):** Gold standard for game fairness
- **iTech Labs:** Tests RNG and game mechanics
- **Gaming Laboratories International (GLI):** Comprehensive testing

**How to verify:** Check for audit badges on the casino's homepage or "Fairness" page.

**Red flag:** No audits mentioned. You're trusting the casino to be honest.

### 7. Fast, Reliable Withdrawals

**What to look for:**
- Withdrawals processed in <24 hours (crypto: <1 hour)
- No "pending" periods longer than 24 hours
- Clear withdrawal limits and fees

**Why it matters:** If withdrawals take weeks, the casino might be stalling (or insolvent).

**Red flags:**
- Withdrawals pending for 5+ days without explanation
- Casino requests endless KYC documents (stalling tactic)
- Withdrawal requests are repeatedly "cancelled" for no reason

### 8. Responsive Customer Support

**What to look for:**
- 24/7 live chat
- Response time <5 minutes
- Knowledgeable support agents (not canned responses)

**Test it:** Before depositing, ask a question via live chat. See how long they take to respond.

**Red flags:**
- Live chat offline during "business hours"
- Support takes 30+ minutes to respond
- Agents can't answer basic questions (RTP, license, withdrawals)

### 9. Transparent Terms and Conditions

**What to look for:**
- Clear withdrawal limits
- Reasonable wagering requirements (no 100x rollovers)
- No hidden fees
- Bonus terms are easy to find

**Red flags:**

- Casino confiscates winnings for "bonus abuse" without clear definition

### 10. Positive Reputation

**What to look for:**

**How to check:**

**Red flags:**

## Provably Fair: How to Verify Crash Games

Provably fair systems let you verify crash games aren't rigged. Here's how it works:

### Step 1: Before the Round

- Casino generates a **server seed** (random number)
- Casino hashes the server seed (SHA-256) and publishes it
- You can't see the server seed yet, but you can verify it later

### Step 2: You Provide a Client Seed

- You choose a **client seed** (any text you want)
- Client seed is combined with server seed to generate the crash result
- You can change your client seed anytime (adds extra randomness)

### Step 3: The Round Plays

- Crash game uses combined seeds to generate the crash multiplier
- You play the round (bet, cashout, win or lose)

### Step 4: After the Round

- Casino reveals the **server seed** (unhashed)
- You verify the server seed matches the hash published before the round
- You recalculate the crash result using both seeds
- If the result matches, the game was fair

**Example verification (Stake Originals):**
1. Go to "Fairness" → "Bet History"
2. Click a past bet → "Verify"
3. Enter the server seed and client seed
4. Verify the calculated crash matches the actual crash

**Red flags:**
- Casino doesn't reveal server seeds
- Can't verify results independently
- Verification tool is missing or broken

**If a crash game isn't provably fair, don't play it.**
