## Beginner Mistakes (1-5)

### Mistake #1: No Auto-Cashout Set

**The error:** Manual cashout ‚Üí timing delays ‚Üí missed multiplier

**How it happens:**
- You see 2x and think "I'll cash out at 3x"
- Game hits 2.8x ‚Üí you hesitate ‚Üí game crashes at 1.01x
- You lose everything because you didn't set auto-cashout

**The cost:**
You crash at 1.01x when you meant to cash out at 2x. **You lose 100% of your intended profit.**

**The math:**
- Without auto-cashout: Reaction time (~0.5-1 second) + Lag (~0.2-0.5 seconds) = Delay
- In 0.7 seconds, crash can go from 2x to 1.01x (game over)
- You missed your multiplier and lost your bet

**Fix:** ALWAYS set auto-cashout before every round. Removes emotion, eliminates lag, guarantees you get your multiplier (or crash trying).

---

### Mistake #2: Betting Your Entire Balance

**The error:** Deposit $100, bet $100 on one round

**How it happens:**
- You deposit $100
- "I'll double it or lose it all" mentality
- Game crashes at 1.00x
- Entire bankroll gone in one round

**The cost:**
One crash at 1.00x = **100% bankroll destruction**. You're broke immediately.

**The math:**
- Crash has ~3% chance of hitting 1.00x (instant crash)
- If you bet your entire bankroll, you have 3% chance of going broke every round
- Play 100 rounds, betting full bankroll each time = **95% probability of ruin**

**Fix:** Never bet more than **5% of your bankroll per round** (1-3% is safer). If you have $100, max bet is $5. This gives you 20+ chances to recover from losses.

### Mistake #3: Chasing Losses Immediately

**The error:** Lose $50, bet $100 to "win it back"

**How it happens:**
- You lose $50
- "I need to make that back fast"
- You double your bet to $100
- Game crashes at 1.05x
- Now you're down $150 (instead of $50)

**The cost:**
Emotional tilt ‚Üí bigger losses ‚Üí **bankroll destruction**. Chasing losses turns a small loss into a catastrophe.

**The math:**
- Lose $50 (5% of $1,000 bankroll)
- Bet $100 to "win it back" (10% of bankroll)
- Lose again: Now down $150 (15% of bankroll)
- Chase again with $200 ‚Üí Lose ‚Üí Down $350 (35% of bankroll)
- In 3 rounds, you turned a 5% loss into a 35% loss

**Fix:** Walk away after a loss. Minimum 30-minute break. Cool down, reset emotions, come back with clear head.

### Mistake #4: Ignoring Provably Fair

**The error:** Playing on unverified crash games

**How it happens:**
- New crash site looks legit
- You deposit and play
- Game keeps crashing at 1.01x (suspicious)
- You realize too late: RNG is rigged

**The cost:**
Casino rigs multipliers, **you lose faster**. Rigged games have 85-90% RTP instead of 97%. You lose 7-12% more per bet.

**The math:**
- Fair game (97% RTP): Expect to lose $3 per $100 bet
- Rigged game (87% RTP): Expect to lose $13 per $100 bet
- **You lose 4x faster** on rigged games

**Fix:** Only play **provably fair crash games**. Verify yourself (run 20-30 bets, use verification tool, confirm results match). If you can't verify, don't play.

### Mistake #5: No Max Loss Limit

**The error:** Play until you're broke

**How it happens:**
- You start session with $200
- "I'll stop when I'm up $100" (but no loss limit)
- You hit a losing streak
- Down $100 ‚Üí Down $150 ‚Üí Down $200
- Now you're broke

**The cost:**
One bad session = **entire bankroll wiped**. No safety net.

**The math:**
- Crash has high variance (streaks happen)
- Without loss limit, variance can wipe you in one session
- Set max loss: 10% of bankroll
- If you have $1,000, stop after losing $100

**Fix:** Set **max loss per session** (10% of bankroll is safe) and **STOP when hit**. No exceptions. Walk away, come back tomorrow.

## Intermediate Mistakes (6-10)

### Mistake #6: The "I'm Due" Fallacy

**The error:** "I lost 5 rounds in a row, I'm due for a win"

**Reality:**
Each crash round is **independent**. Past results don't influence future outcomes.

**How it happens:**
- You lose 5 rounds in a row
- "Probability says I should win soon"
- You bet bigger because "I'm due"
- Game crashes at 1.01x again
- Now you're down even more

**The cost:**
Betting bigger because you "feel due" ‚Üí **bigger losses**. Each round is 50/50 (regardless of past).

**The math:**
- Crash game has no memory
- Round 1: 3% house edge
- Round 2: 3% house edge (independent of round 1)
- Round 100: 3% house edge (independent of rounds 1-99)
- You're never "due" ‚Äî probability doesn't work that way

**Fix:** Accept each round is independent. Don't increase bets after losses. Stick to your strategy (fixed % of bankroll per bet).

### Mistake #7: Cashing Out Too Late (Greed)

**The error:** Auto-cashout at 2x, you see 3x and cancel to chase 5x

**How it happens:**
- Auto-cashout set to 2x
- Game passes 2x, heading to 3x
- "I can get 5x if I wait a bit longer"
- You cancel auto-cashout
- Game crashes at 1.05x
- You lose everything

**The cost:**
Greed kills profits. You had guaranteed 2x win, but got greedy and lost everything.

**The math:**
- Cancel auto-cashout at 3x to chase 5x
- 80% of rounds crash before 5x (you'll lose most of the time)
- Over 100 rounds: You'd win 2x √ó 100 = $200 (if you stuck to plan)
- Chasing 5x: You'd win 5x √ó 20 (wins) - $1 √ó 80 (losses) = $100 - $80 = $20 profit
- **Greed cost you 90% of your profit**

**Fix:** Set auto-cashout and **NEVER touch it**. Discipline > greed. If you want higher multipliers, set auto-cashout higher from the start ‚Äî don't cancel mid-round.

### Mistake #8: Cashing Out Too Early (Fear)

**The error:** Auto-cashout at 1.5x when you planned 3x

**How it happens:**
- Auto-cashout set to 3x
- Game hits 1.5x, you see it dipped
- "Better take profit now"
- You manually cash out at 1.5x
- Next round, game goes to 4x (you missed out)

**The cost:**
Fear causes **missed opportunities**. Slow bankroll growth.

**The math:**
- Plan: Cash out at 3x (wins ~33% of time)
- Fear: Cash out at 1.5x (wins ~67% of time)
- But 3x √ó 33% = 1.0x average return
- 1.5x √ó 67% = 1.0x average return
- Same expected value, but 3x has higher variance (bigger wins)
- Cashing out early = slower bankroll growth

**Fix:** Trust your strategy. Don't let fear override math. If you planned 3x, stick to 3x.

### Mistake #9: Playing Drunk or High

**The error:** Impaired judgment ‚Üí bad decisions

**How it happens:**
- You're drunk/high
- "I feel lucky, let's bet big"
- You bet 20% of your bankroll
- Game crashes at 1.01x
- You tilt and chase losses
- Wake up next morning: Broke

**The cost:**
Impaired gambling = **faster losses, bigger mistakes**. You ignore auto-cashout, bet too big, chase losses.

**The math:**
- Sober: You make 1 mistake per 100 rounds
- Drunk: You make 10 mistakes per 100 rounds (10x more errors)
- Each mistake costs 10-50% of your bankroll
- Drunk gambling: You lose 5-10x faster

**Fix:** Only play sober. Crash requires precision (timing, discipline, math). Impairment destroys all three.

### Mistake #10: No Strategy, Just "Feel"

**The error:** Betting random amounts, cashing out randomly

**How it happens:**
- No strategy, just "going with the flow"
- Round 1: Bet $5, cash out at 2x
- Round 2: Bet $20, cash out at 1.5x
- Round 3: Bet $2, cash out at 5x
- No consistency, no math, just random

**The cost:**
Volatile swings, **no edge**, faster losses. Random betting = guaranteed long-term loss.

**The math:**
- Crash has 3% house edge
- Random betting: You're playing a -EV game with no strategy
- Expected loss: 3% of every bet (long-term)
- Bet $1,000 over 100 rounds ‚Üí Expect to lose $30

**Fix:** Use a **math-backed strategy** (Fibonacci, fixed-wager, conservative). All crash strategies are -EV long-term, but having a strategy reduces variance and prevents emotional mistakes.

## Advanced Mistakes (11-15)

### Mistake #11: Overestimating Your Edge

**The error:** "I have a winning strategy" (you don't)

**Reality:**
Even optimal strategy = **-3% EV per bet**. House always wins.

**How it happens:**
- You win $500 in one session
- "I've figured this game out"
- You increase bet sizes
- Variance swings the other way
- You lose $1,000 next session

**The cost:**
Believing you have an "edge" ‚Üí **betting bigger** ‚Üí ruin. You're not profitable; you just got lucky.

**The math:**
- Crash RTP: 97% (3% house edge)
- Optimal strategy: -3% EV per bet (you can't overcome this)
- Win $500 in one session = Variance (not skill)
- Over 1,000 rounds: You'll lose ~3% of total wagered (guaranteed)

**Fix:** Accept you're playing for **fun, not profit**. If you can't accept this, stop playing. No crash strategy is profitable long-term.

### Mistake #12: Variance Blindness

**The error:** "I won $500 in one hour, I'm profitable!"

**Reality:**
Short-term wins mean nothing. Variance swings both ways.

**How it happens:**
- You play for 1 hour, win $500
- "I'm beating the game!"
- You withdraw, thinking you're a pro
- Next week: You lose $800 in 2 hours
- Net: -$300 (but you only remember the $500 win)

**The cost:**
Withdrawing too early, thinking you're profitable. You're not ‚Äî variance gave you a temporary win.

**The math:**
- Play 100 rounds: Variance is huge (you could win or lose big)
- Play 1,000 rounds: Variance smooths out (you'll approach -3% EV)
- Play 10,000 rounds: You'll definitely lose ~3% of total wagered
- Your 1-hour win = Variance, not skill

**Fix:** Track results over **1,000+ rounds** (not 100). If you're profitable after 1,000 rounds, then you can talk (but you probably won't be).

### Mistake #13: Playing Too Long

**The error:** 4-hour sessions ‚Üí fatigue ‚Üí mistakes

**How it happens:**
- You start fresh, playing well
- 2 hours in: Getting tired
- 3 hours in: Making small mistakes
- 4 hours in: One big mistake = wipe out 2 hours of wins

**The cost:**
Fatigue causes mistakes. One mistake after 3 hours can **undo 2 hours of profit**.

**The math:**
- Fresh: You make 1 mistake per 100 rounds
- After 3 hours: You make 5 mistakes per 100 rounds (fatigue)
- Each mistake costs 20-50% of one bet
- Playing too long = Mistake cost > Session profit

**Fix:** **Max 1-hour sessions**. Take breaks (10 minutes every hour). Stop when tired. Fatigue = expensive.

### Mistake #14: Multi-Tabling Crash

**The error:** Playing 3 crash games at once

**How it happens:**
- You have Stake crash, BC.Game crash, Roobet crash open
- Trying to "spread action"
- One game hits 2x, you miss it (watching another game)
- Another game crashes at 1.01x, you don't see it coming
- Confusion, missed multipliers, faster losses

**The cost:**
Missed multipliers, confusion, **3x expected loss per hour**. You're not 3x profitable; you're 3x losing.

**The math:**
- Play 1 game: Lose $30/hour (3% edge)
- Play 3 games: Lose $90/hour (same edge, 3x action)
- But confusion causes 2x more mistakes
- Actual loss: $180/hour (6x single-game loss)

**Fix:** **One game at a time**. Crash requires focus. Multi-tabling = guaranteed losses.

### Mistake #15: Believing "Hot" or "Cold" Games

**The error:** "This game is hot right now!" or "It's due to crash at 10x!"

**Reality:**
Crash is random. No patterns, no hot/cold, no "due" outcomes.

**How it happens:**
- Game hits 5x three rounds in a row
- "This game is hot! Bet big!"
- You bet $100 (instead of $10)
- Game crashes at 1.01x next round
- You lose 10x your usual bet

**The cost:**
Chasing patterns = **losing strategy**. Randomness doesn't have patterns.

**The math:**
- Each round is independent (3% house edge)
- "Hot" streaks = Variance (not a pattern)
- Betting bigger during "hot" streaks = You lose more when variance reverses
- "Cold" streaks = Same (each round is still independent)

**Fix:** Ignore patterns. Each round is 50/50. Bet the same amount every round (fixed % of bankroll).
