## How Crash Gambling Works: The Math Behind the Multiplier

### The Crash Game Mechanism

Crash gambling is simple: **place a bet, watch the multiplier climb, cash out before it crashes.**

But what determines when the crash happens? That's where the math matters.

### The House Edge: Every Crash Game Has One

Here's the uncomfortable truth: **Crash games are designed to favor the casino in the long run.**

The house edge typically ranges from **1% to 4%**. This means:
- For every $100 wagered, the casino expects to keep $1‚Äì$4
- You can win in the short term, but the math guarantees the casino profits over time

**Example:** A crash game with a 1% house edge and a 2.00x target multiplier:
- Your win probability: 49.5% (not 50%)
- Casino edge: 1% baked into the math
- Over 1,000 rounds at $10 each, you'll lose ~$100 on average

**This isn't rigging ‚Äî it's math.** All casino games have a house edge. Crash is no different.

### The RNG (Random Number Generator)

Legitimate crash games use **cryptographically secure random number generators** to determine each round's multiplier:

1. **Server seed:** A random number generated by the casino (hashed and revealed later)
2. **Client seed:** A random number generated by your browser (you can customize this)
3. **Nonce:** A counter that increments each round

The crash point = `hash(server seed + client seed + nonce)`

This creates a **verifiable random outcome** that neither you nor the casino can predict or manipulate.
