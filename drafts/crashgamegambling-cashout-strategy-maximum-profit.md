## Understanding Crash Cashout Mechanics

Before diving into strategies, you need to understand how crash games work under the hood. Every crash game uses a **provably random number generator (RNG)** that determines when the multiplier will crash. The key insight: each round is independent, completely random, and follows a specific mathematical distribution.

### How Crash Multipliers Work

Most crash games use a modified version of the following algorithm:
1. The server generates a random number between 0 and 1
2. This number is converted to a crash point using an exponential distribution
3. The house edge (typically 3-5%) is built into this formula
4. The result is your crash multiplier (e.g., 1.45x, 2.3x, 8.92x)

**What this means for your strategy:** Past results do not influence future rounds. A game that just crashed at 1.01x has the same probability of hitting 100x as any other round. This is why "chasing losses" or betting on "hot streaks" is mathematically flawed.

### The House Edge Reality

Here's the hard truth: crash games are designed to favor the casino over time. With a typical 4% house edge, for every $100 wagered, you can expect to lose $4 in the long run. Your cashout strategy cannot overcome this mathematical realityâ€”but it can maximize your chances of walking away a winner during a session.

The goal isn't to beat the house edge (impossible). The goal is to manage variance and capitalize on short-term positive fluctuations.

**Risk-Reward Ratio = (Probability of Loss) / (Probability of Win Ã— (Multiplier - 1))**
