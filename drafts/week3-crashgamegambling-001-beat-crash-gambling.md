## The Hard Truth: House Edge Always Wins

Crash games use a simple formula:

```
Multiplier = (0.99 / Random Number Between 0 and 1)
```

That 0.99? That's the house edge. It means the game pays out 99% of all bets over time. The casino keeps 1%.

Doesn't sound like much? Here's what 1% looks like in practice:

- **$100 per bet** ‚Üí You lose $1 per bet, on average
- **100 bets per hour** ‚Üí You lose $100 per hour

This is **Expected Value (EV)**. Every crash game has negative EV for players. That's how casinos stay in business.

**No strategy changes this.** No betting system, no pattern spotting, no "predictor bot" can flip negative EV positive.

What you *can* control:

### 2. Bankroll Management (1-2% Per Bet)

**The most important strategy. Period.**

Your bet size determines how long you play. Bet too big, you bust out fast. Bet small, you survive variance.

**The 1% Rule:**
Never bet more than 1% of your total bankroll per round.

**Example:**
- Bankroll: $500

- Even a 10-bet losing streak only loses $50 (10% of bankroll)

**The 2% Rule (Aggressive):**
Never bet more than 2% per round. Only for experienced players with larger bankrolls.

**Why this matters:**
Crash is high volatility. You *will* have losing streaks. Bankroll management ensures you survive them.

With 1% betting, a 10-loss streak costs 10% of your bankroll. Survivable.
With 10% betting, a 10-loss streak wipes you out. Game over.

## Strategies That DON'T Work (Stay Away)

These "strategies" are myths, scams, or mathematically flawed. Avoid them.

### ‚ùå Pattern Spotting

**The myth:** "Crash has patterns! After three 2x crashes, a big one is due!"

**The reality:** Every crash round is **independent**. Previous rounds have zero effect on future rounds.

**Why it feels real:**
- Confirmation bias (you remember the "hits," forget the misses)
- Gambler's fallacy (believing past outcomes influence future ones)
- Randomness naturally produces "streaks" (it's not a pattern, it's variance)

**Example:**
If crash hit 1.1x three times in a row, what's the probability it hits 1.1x again?
- **Answer:** Exactly the same as always (~9% at 1.1x)

The game has no memory. No "due" multipliers. No patterns.

### ‚ùå Martingale (Double After Loss)

**The strategy:** Double your bet after every loss. When you finally win, you recover all losses plus profit.

**Example:**
- Bet $10, lose
- Bet $20, lose
- Bet $40, lose
- Bet $80, win ‚Üí You're up $10 ($160 win - $150 loss)

**The problem:**

**The reality:**
- 10-loss streak at $10 start bet = **$10,240 wagered** to recover
- Most players bust before they win

**Does it work?** No. It just rearranges variance into one catastrophic loss instead of many small ones.

### ‚ùå Fibonacci System

**The strategy:** Follow the Fibonacci sequence (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13...) for bet sizing. Increase after losses, decrease after wins.

**The reality:** Still negative EV. Still doesn't change the house edge. Just a more "gentle" Martingale.

## Expected Value Math: Why You Can't Win

**Expected Value (EV)** is the average amount you'll win or lose per bet.

**Formula:**
```
EV = (Probability of Win √ó Win Amount) - (Probability of Loss √ó Loss Amount)
```

**Example: 2x cashout strategy**
- Win probability: ~50% (actually 49.5% due to house edge)
- Loss probability: ~50.5%
- Win amount: $10 (you bet $10, win $10 profit at 2x)
- Loss amount: $10

```
EV = (0.495 √ó $10) - (0.505 √ó $10)
EV = $4.95 - $5.05
EV = -$0.10 per $10 bet
```

**You lose 10 cents per bet.** Over 1,000 bets? You lose $100.

**Example: 10x cashout strategy**
- Win probability: ~9% (actually 8.9%)
- Loss probability: ~91.1%
- Win amount: $90 (you bet $10, win $90 profit at 10x)

```
EV = (0.089 √ó $90) - (0.911 √ó $10)
EV = $8.01 - $9.11
EV = -$1.10 per $10 bet
```

**You lose $1.10 per bet.** Over 1,000 bets? You lose $1,100.

**Chasing big multipliers = losing faster.** The math doesn't care about your feelings.
