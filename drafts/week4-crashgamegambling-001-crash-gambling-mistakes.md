## Beginner Mistakes (1-5)

### **Mistake #1: No Auto-Cashout Set**

**The error:**
You play manually, waiting for the "right moment" to cash out. But by the time you react, the game has already crashed. You meant to cash out at 2x, but hesitated for 0.2 seconds — and crashed at 1.01x.

**The cost:**
You miss 50% of your intended profit. If you aimed for 2x but average 1.01x due to timing delays, you're losing HALF your expected value.

**The math:**
- Intended cashout: 2x
- Average actual cashout (manual delay): 1.3x
- Lost profit: 35% per bet

Over 100 bets at $10 each:
- Expected profit (perfect 2x): $1,000
- Actual profit (manual 1.3x): $300
- **Lost to timing: $700**

**The fix:**
ALWAYS set auto-cashout. It removes emotion, reduces lag, guarantees your target multiplier. Auto-cashout at 2x means you cash out at 2x — not 1.3x, not 1.01x.

**Example:**
- Set auto-cashout: 2x
- Bet: $10
- Result: $20 profit (every time you win)

No emotion, no hesitation, no lost profit.

### **Mistake #2: Betting Your Entire Balance**

**The error:**
You deposit $100, bet $100 on one round, hoping for a quick 2x. One crash at 1.00x — and your entire bankroll is gone.

**The cost:**
Bankroll destruction. One round = bust. You have to deposit again (chasing losses, which leads to more mistakes).

**The math:**
At $100 bets with $100 bankroll:
- One loss = 100% bankroll gone
- Probability of bust within 10 rounds: **90%+**

**The fix:**
Never bet more than 5% of your bankroll per round.

**Example:**

- One loss = 5% gone, 95% remaining
- You can survive 20 consecutive losses before busting

**Bankroll management > strategy.**

**The cost:**
Emotional tilt → bigger bets → faster losses. Chasing losses doesn't work — it multiplies them.

**The math:**
- Starting bet: $50
- Loss: -$50
- Chase bet: $100 (lose again)
- Chase bet: $200 (lose again)
- Total lost: $350 in 3 rounds

If you'd stopped at -$50 and taken a break, you'd have $50 left. Instead, you're down $350.

**The fix:**
Walk away after a loss. Minimum 30-minute break. No exceptions.

**Reality:** Crash rounds are independent. Past losses don't increase your odds of winning. Chasing is pure emotion, not logic.

## Intermediate Mistakes (6-10)

### **Mistake #6: The "I'm Due" Fallacy**

**The error:**
You lost 5 rounds in a row and think, "I'm due for a win." You bet bigger because "the odds must turn in my favor."

**Reality:**
Each crash round is independent. Past results don't influence future outcomes. You're not "due" for anything.

**The math:**
- 5 losses in a row: Common (happens 3% of the time)
- 6th round probability: Still 50/50 (coin flip)
- Believing you're "due" = betting bigger = losing bigger

**Example:**
- Base bet: $10
- Lost 5 rounds: -$50
- "I'm due" bet: $50 (bigger to "catch up")
- 6th round loses: -$100 total

If you'd stuck to $10 bets, you'd be down $60. Chasing "due" wins cost you an extra $40.

**The fix:**
Accept independence. Each round is 50/50. No patterns, no "due" outcomes. Flat bet or use a strategy — don't increase bets because of past results.

**The math:**
- Auto-cashout at 2x: Guaranteed 2x (when you win)

**Example:**
- Bet: $10

- Cancel to chase 10x: Crash at 1.05x = $10 loss
- **Greed cost: $30** (potential profit + actual loss)

**The fix:**
Set auto-cashout and NEVER touch it. Discipline > greed.

If you want higher multipliers, set auto-cashout higher from the start. Don't adjust mid-round — that's pure emotion.

### **Mistake #12: Variance Blindness**

**The error:**

- Session 3: -$300 (house edge kicks in)
- **Net: -$200** (long-term reality)

**The fix:**
Track results over 1,000+ rounds, not 100. Short-term variance is noise — long-term EV is signal.
