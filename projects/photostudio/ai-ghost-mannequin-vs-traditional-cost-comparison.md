## Traditional Fashion Photography: The Hidden Costs

### Studio Setup and Equipment

Traditional photography requires significant upfront investment:

- **Professional camera gear:** $3,000-$10,000 for DSLR/mirrorless bodies, lenses, lighting
- **Studio space:** $500-$5,000/month (depending on location)
- **Lighting equipment:** $1,000-$5,000 for softboxes, strobes, modifiers
- **Mannequins:** $200-$1,000 per quality mannequin (multiple needed for different sizes)
- **Backgrounds and props:** $500-$2,000 initial setup

### Per-Product Photography Costs

For each product, traditional photography involves:

- **Photographer fees:** $50-$200 per hour (2-4 hours per product average)
- **Photo editing:** $30-$100 per image (ghost mannequin effect, retouching)
- **Mannequin setup/styling:** $20-$50 per product
- **Multiple shots needed:** Ghost mannequin, flatlay, detail shots, lifestyle

**Total per product:** $150-$500+ for a complete photo set

### Ongoing Expenses

- **Product returns:** When products sell out or change, you need new photos
- **Seasonal updates:** New collections require full photography cycles
- **A/B testing:** Different angles, styling, or backgrounds mean reshoots
- **Model fees:** On-model photography adds $100-$500 per session

## When Traditional Photography Still Makes Sense

AI isn't always the answer. Traditional photography excels for:

- **Campaign shoots:** Editorial content, brand storytelling
- **Complex products:** Jewelry, highly reflective materials
- **Custom styling:** Unique artistic vision requiring human creativity
- **Print advertising:** Extremely high-resolution requirements

But for day-to-day e-commerce product photography? AI wins on cost, speed, and flexibility.
